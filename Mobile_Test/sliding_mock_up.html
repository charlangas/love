About CanIGetMyOrderForFreeAtTheBodyShopYet.com


Oh Joy!
Equally famous for their fantastic body care products as for their strong adherence to environmental and social ethics, The Body Shop has gained an intensely loyal following since it was founded in 1976. During the 2010 Holiday season, POKE joined the The Body Shop to spread the joy among its fans with an innovative online promotion.


We don't know, can you?
For 38 consecutive days, fans could visit the single-page website we created at this memorable URL: CanIGetMyOrderForFreeAtThe
BodyShopYet.com. Once a day at a pre-determined time, one lucky visitor would see a bold "YES" in place of the usual "NO," earning $100 off their order and plenty, plenty of joy just in time to get their Holiday shopping done.


Wanna get lucky?
Over the 38 days that the promotion lasted, our fans developed several strategies for winning the daily prize, including refreshing the page over 200 times in a row and visiting it at random times throughout the day. Fans that followed The Body Shop on Twitter and Facebook could also get clues every day, hinting the time when the prize would be released.

About The Body Shop Holiday Joy Campaign 2011


About the idea
For the second year in a row, we worked with The Body Shop to create their Holiday Joy campaign, which gave fans the chance to win a free order (up to $100) just by visiting this fantastically fun URL: CanIGetMyOrderForFree
AtTheBodyShopYet.com. Once a day at a pre-determined time, one lucky winner could refresh the page to see a bold "YES" in the snow globe in place of the usual "NO", and five times a day, a fan could win a free body butter.

New for 2011
One thing was clear from the 2010 campaign: People loved to visit the site ? hundreds of page views at a time, in fact. (Not bad for a one page site.) So this year we created a snow globe that visitors could "shake" virtually on their computer, or literally on their iPhone. Regardless if they saw a "YES" or a "NO", fans could donate their accumulated shakes to charity. More than a million shakes were donated and the charity with the highest total received $1,000 in products from The Body Shop.
